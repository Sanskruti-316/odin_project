Puran Poli Recipe

About Puran Poli

Puran Poli is a traditional Indian sweet flatbread made with a filling of jaggery and chana dal (split Bengal gram).
It is commonly prepared during festivals and special occasions in various regions of India, particularly in Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Karnataka.
The dish is known for its delightful combination of flavors, with the sweetness of jaggery complementing the earthy taste of chana dal.
Puran Poli is typically served warm, often accompanied by ghee (clarified butter) or milk, making it a comforting and indulgent treat
.
  • Ingredients for Puran Poli:
    • 1 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
    • 1 cup jaggery (grated)
    • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
    • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • Water as needed
    • A pinch of salt
  • Instructions to make Puran Poli:
    • Rinse the chana dal and cook it in a pressure cooker with enough water until soft. Drain any excess water.
    • In a pan, add the cooked chana dal and jaggery. Cook on low heat until the jaggery melts and combines with the dal. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
    • Add cardamom powder to the mixture and cook until it thickens. Let it cool.
    • In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, turmeric powder, a pinch of salt, and oil/ghee. Add water gradually to form a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    • Divide the dough and the puran mixture into equal portions.
    • Roll out a portion of dough into a small circle. Place a portion of puran in the center and fold the edges over to seal it.
    • Gently roll out the stuffed dough into a flat circle (poli).
    • Heat a tawa or skillet and cook the puran poli on both sides until golden brown, applying ghee as needed.
    • Serve warm with ghee or milk.

    Enjoy your delicious homemade Puran Poli!
