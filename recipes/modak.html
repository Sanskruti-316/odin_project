Modak Recipe

About Modak

The traditional steamed Modak is a sweet dumpling with an outer rice flour dough and a coconut-jaggery stuffing.
These dumplings are also known as Ukadiche Modak in Marathi language.
It is a popular sweet that is commonly prepared and enjoyed in India, especially during the Hindu festival of Ganesh Chaturthi.
  • Ingredients for Modak:
    • 1 cup rice flour
    • 1 cup grated coconut
    • 3/4 cup jaggery (grated)
    • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
    • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
    • Water as needed
    • A pinch of salt
  • Instructions to make Modak:
    • In a pan, heat ghee and add grated coconut and jaggery. Cook until the jaggery melts and combines with the coconut. Add cardamom powder and mix well. Let it cool.
    • In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, a pinch of salt, and hot water to form a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 15 minutes.
    • Divide the dough and the coconut-jaggery mixture into equal portions.
    • Roll out a portion of dough into a small circle. Place a portion of the coconut-jaggery mixture in the center and fold the edges to form a modak shape.
    • Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
    • Steam the modaks in a steamer for about 10-15 minutes until they are cooked through.
    • Serve warm with ghee.

    Enjoy your delicious homemade Modak!
